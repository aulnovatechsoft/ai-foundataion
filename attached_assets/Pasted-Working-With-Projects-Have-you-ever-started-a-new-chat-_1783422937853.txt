Working With Projects
Have you ever started a new chat to continue working on something, only to realize that AI doesn't remember what you discussed before?
You end up re-explaining your goals, repeating constraints, and rebuilding context from scratch. The more complex the task, the more frustrating this gets.
Claude's Projects is a feature designed to solve exactly this. Let's see how it works!

A Different Approach
This is where Projects come in. It is a dedicated workspace where you set up your context once, and Claude carries it across every conversation inside it.

To create a project, go to Projects → New Project, give it a name and a short description of what it's for.

Then, there are two spaces to fill:
Instructions: your goals, constraints, and preferences in plain language.
Files, where you can attach any documents related to your work.
On higher-tier plans, a Project also has a "Memory" block, where Claude can hold onto details it learns as you work — and that Memory stays scoped to just this Project. If you don't see it, don't worry: your instructions and files already carry the context that matters most.

As a result, every time you start a new chat inside the Project, Claude will automatically see those instructions and files. You don't paste them again — they're just there.

It's like giving Claude a briefing before every conversation, without actually having to brief it every time.
In the context of renovation planning, this approach will make Claude keep and always see:
The overall goal
Previous decisions
Constraints you've already discussed

Setting Up a Project
To set up a Project, start by describing context in the "Instructions" block.

For example:
The size of the apartment
Your rough budget range
The rooms involved
What matters most (cost, speed, quality, or style)
It will become the foundation that everything else builds on.

In addition, you can also break the plan into phases and instruct Claude on:
What should happen first
Which steps depend on others
Where decisions can wait
This will help Claude memorize the overall plan and respond effectively in every chat inside the Project.
To make this clearer, imagine you want to discuss flooring and lighting separately without mixing everything into one long chat.

With a Project set up, you can simply open a new chat inside it for each phase:
One for flooring
Another for lighting
A third one for plumbing
Each chat stays focused, but Claude will still know your overall budget, priorities, and earlier decisions.

This gives you two things at once:
You're no longer re-explaining your full situation every time you ask a question — Claude already has the context.
You keep everything organized, with each phase in its own conversation instead of buried in one endless thread.

That's the core idea behind Projects: they're not a one-time setup you need to get perfect — they're a living workspace where context builds up gradually, and priorities can shift.

Claude keeps up without losing the thread.

Projects Aren't the Same Everywhere
You may have seen a Projects feature in other tools like ChatGPT. The basics rhyme — group conversations, add instructions and files — but Claude leans hard on continuity: earlier decisions in a Project actively shape later suggestions, so the work compounds rather than just staying organized.
Where Claude's Projects Shine
Claude's Projects feel strongest when the work itself is evolving. They're especially useful for:
Planning that requires revisiting assumptions
Long-form writing with a consistent direction
Learning complex topics over time
Comparing options and tradeoffs repeatedly
In these cases, continuity isn't just convenient — it actively improves the quality of thinking.
